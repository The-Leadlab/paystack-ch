Introduction

This report extends the Swiss budgeting competitor analysis by mapping each feature category to Paystack.ch — a Swiss hospitality & finance SaaS with AI document processing, business ledger, Swiss TVA, and personal household budgeting at /app/personal.

Key difference: Paystack does not use bank sync (bLink / Open Banking). Transactions enter via AI document upload or manual entry — aligned with restaurant and SME workflows, not multibanking aggregation.

Paystack modules — implementation detail

Business platform (/app) — Implemented

  • Firebase authentication, Stripe subscriptions, plan limits (docs/month, sessions, employees)
  • Session-based ledger: income, expenses, drag-and-drop convert
  • AI document processor (batch upload, Swiss plan comptable account codes)
  • Swiss TVA tracking and export (Business plan)
  • Swiss payroll extraction and split on payslips
  • Reports: CSV/PDF; upgrade prompt at document cap
  • POS / Z-reading module (partial)

Personal finances (/app/personal) — Implemented

  • Overview dashboard, budget vs actual, zero-based mode
  • Goals, bill reminders, 90-day forecast, investment portfolio
  • Shared Firebase ledger with Business — add/edit/delete transactions
  • EN / FR / DE / IT in personal UI (lab i18n merging to app)

In progress — shipping next

Pending — roadmap

  • Inventory (FIFO/LIFO), supplier management, employee HR module
  • Client & project management
  • Multi-currency accounts, retirement planner, auto-budget from history

Out of scope (by design)

  • Bank synchronization (bLink, PostFinance/UBS API, CSV bank import)

Conclusion

For Swiss users who want privacy-first manual budgeting, BudgetCH remains the free benchmark. Paystack competes on AI-powered document workflows, business + personal in one ledger, and Swiss compliance (TVA, payroll, CHF) — not on bank aggregation.

Personal finance features (YNAB-style zero-based budgeting, Buxfer-style forecast & investments, BlueBudget-style bill reminders) are implemented at /app/personal. Invoice creation is the next major gap being closed for SMEs.
